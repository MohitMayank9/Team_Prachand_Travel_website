AGRA

Agra, a city in Uttar Pradesh, India, is renowned for its rich history and architectural marvels, most notably the iconic Taj Mahal. Here are some famous places to visit in Agra:.

Taj Mahal: A UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the Seven Wonders of the World, the Taj Mahal is a breathtaking monument built by Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal. Its white marble façade, intricate carvings, and symmetrical gardens make it a symbol of eternal love.

Agra Fort: Another UNESCO World Heritage Site, the Agra Fort is a majestic red sandstone fortress that served as the main residence of the Mughal emperors until 1638. It features impressive palaces, courtyards, and mosques, including the Jahangir Palace and the Moti Masjid.

Fatehpur Sikri: Located just outside Agra, Fatehpur Sikri is a well-preserved Mughal city built by Emperor Akbar in the 16th century. It features stunning architecture, including the Buland Darwaza, Jama Masjid, and the tomb of Salim Chishti.

Itmad-ud-Daulah's Tomb: Often referred to as the "Baby Taj" due to its resemblance to the Taj Mahal, this tomb was built for Mirza Ghiyas Beg by his daughter Nur Jahan. It is known for its intricate marble inlay work and serene surroundings.

Akbar's Tomb, Sikandra: This grand mausoleum houses the tomb of Emperor Akbar and is a blend of Mughal and Hindu architectural styles. The complex includes beautiful gardens and reflects the emperor's secular beliefs.

Agra Itinerary

Day 1: Taj Mahal

Witness the breathtaking beauty of the Taj Mahal, one of the Seven Wonders of the World.

Day 2: Jama Masjid

Explore the grandeur of Jama Masjid, one of the largest mosques in India, known for its intricate architecture.

Day 3: Fatehpur Sikri

Visit the UNESCO World Heritage Site of Fatehpur Sikri, a deserted Mughal city with stunning architecture.

Day 4: Mankameshwar Temple

Experience the spirituality of Agra at Mankameshwar Temple, dedicated to Lord Shiva.

Taj Mahal

Taj Mahal: A UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the Seven Wonders of the World, the Taj Mahal is a breathtaking monument built by Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal. Its white marble façade, intricate carvings, and symmetrical gardens make it a symbol of eternal love..

Jama Masjid

Gur Jama Masjid, located in Agra, Uttar Pradesh, India, is one of the city's most prominent landmarks and largest mosques. Commissioned by Emperor Shah Jahan in the 17th century, it stands as a testament to the architectural brilliance and religious tolerance of the Mughal era.

Fatehpur Sikri

Fatehpur Sikri: Located just outside Agra, Fatehpur Sikri is a well-preserved Mughal city built by Emperor Akbar in the 16th century. It features stunning architecture, including the Buland Darwaza, Jama Masjid, and the tomb of Salim Chishti.

Mankameshwar Temple

Mankameshwar Temple: Situated near the Jama Masjid, Mankameshwar Temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva and is believed to fulfill the wishes of devotees. The temple complex includes shrines dedicated to various Hindu deities and attracts devotees seeking blessings and spiritual solace..
